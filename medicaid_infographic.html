üéØ Key Insight: The Coverage vs. Usage Gap

While Medicaid expansion successfully increased insurance coverage and formal primary care relationships, it did not translate into increased real-world usage of primary care services. This suggests systemic barriers beyond insurance coverage may be limiting access.

Policy Implications

Recommended Solutions

  • Incentivize Primary Care Careers: Provide federal grants and signing bonuses to encourage medical students to choose primary care
  • Address Geographic Gaps: Offer relocation assistance to primary care providers willing to work in rural and underserved areas
  • Public Education Campaign: Develop targeted outreach highlighting benefits of primary care and where to find services

Study Limitations & Future Work

Limitations: State-level variation in Medicaid expansion implementation, potential confounding factors, and geographic data restrictions.

Future Research: Incorporate state fixed effects, collect primary survey data, and use machine learning clustering techniques for more granular analysis.
